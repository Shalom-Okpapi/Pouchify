Copyright Basics

Copyright is legal protection for original creative works. Understanding these fundamentals will help you protect your work and respect others':

What's Protected

  • Original artwork and designs
  • Photographs you've taken
  • Illustrations and graphics
  • Literary works (text/poetry)
  • Original characters

What's Not Protected

  • Ideas, concepts, or styles
  • Common symbols or shapes
  • Facts or basic information
  • Titles, names, or short phrases
  • Works in the public domain

Automatic Protection

In most countries, copyright protection is automatic when you create an original work. You don't need to register or include a copyright notice (though it can help). Your work is protected whether you post it online or keep it private.

Your Rights as a Creator

When you upload original work to Pouchify, you retain important rights:

You Keep Copyright

Uploading to Pouchify doesn't transfer your copyright. You maintain ownership of your original work.

Limited License

You grant Pouchify a license to reproduce and sell your designs on products. This license is non-exclusive (you can sell elsewhere too).

Control Over Your Work

You can edit or remove your designs anytime. Deleted designs will be removed from sale.

Protection Against Infringement

Pouchify respects copyright law and will remove infringing copies of your work when properly reported.

Watermarks & Protection

While we discourage visible watermarks (they detract from your art), consider these protection methods:

  • Upload lower-resolution preview images
  • Add subtle signature elements in your design
  • Register important works with copyright offices
  • Monitor for unauthorized use regularly

Prohibited Content

To respect intellectual property rights and comply with law, Pouchify prohibits:

Category Examples Why Prohibited
Trademarks Brand names, logos, slogans Unauthorized commercial use
Copyrighted Characters Naruto, Harry Potter Requires license from owner
Celebrity Images Photos of actors, musicians Right of publicity violations
Sports Team IP Team logos, player likenesses Trademark and publicity rights
Movie/TV Content Screenshots, promotional art Copyrighted by studios

Common Mistakes

Even with good intentions, artists sometimes accidentally infringe by:

  • Using fonts without commercial licenses
  • Incorporating stock images beyond their terms
  • Referencing brands through look-alike designs
  • Creating "inspired by" works that are too similar

Fan Art Policy

Fan art occupies a gray area in copyright law. Our policy balances legal requirements with creative expression:

Allowed Fan Art

  • Highly original interpretations
  • Transformative works (new style/medium)
  • Parodies with clear humorous intent
  • Works sufficiently different from source

Prohibited Fan Art

  • Direct copies of copyrighted characters
  • Use of trademarked names/logos
  • Minimally altered existing artwork
  • Works likely to confuse consumers

Best Practices for Fan Art

  1. Make it original: Your unique style should dominate
  2. Avoid trademarks: No logos, team names, or brand identifiers
  3. Be transformative: Add significant new creative elements
  4. Consider fair use: Educate yourself on this legal doctrine
  5. Get permission: Some copyright owners offer fan art licenses

Reporting Violations

If you believe someone has infringed your copyright, or if your work was mistakenly removed:

Gather Evidence

Collect proof of your original work and the alleged infringement. Dates are important.

Submit DMCA Notice

Use our DMCA form to file an official takedown request. Include all required information.

Counter-Notification

If your work was removed in error, you may file a counter-notice explaining why.

Resolution

Pouchify will review the claim and respond according to copyright law.

False Claims Warning

Knowingly submitting false copyright claims can result in:

  • Legal consequences under DMCA
  • Termination of your Pouchify account
  • Potential liability for damages

Only file claims for works you genuinely created.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to register my copyright to be protected?

No, copyright protection is automatic when you create an original work. However, registration (available through your country's copyright office) provides additional legal benefits if you need to file a lawsuit for infringement.

Can I use Creative Commons or stock images in my designs?

It depends on the specific license:

  • CC-BY: Usually allowed with proper attribution
  • CC-NC: Not allowed (Pouchify is commercial)
  • Stock images: Check license terms carefully
  • Public domain: Generally acceptable

When in doubt, assume you need explicit permission.

What happens if I receive a copyright takedown notice?

If your work is removed due to a DMCA notice:

  1. You'll receive an email with details
  2. The design will be deactivated
  3. You can file a counter-notice if you believe it was a mistake
  4. Repeat infringements may lead to account suspension

How can I legally use copyrighted material?

You may use copyrighted material if:

  • You obtain permission from the copyright owner
  • Your use qualifies as fair use (varies by jurisdiction)
  • The material is licensed for commercial use
  • The copyright has expired (varies by country)

When in doubt, consult an intellectual property attorney.
