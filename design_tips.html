File Preparation

Start with high-quality files to ensure your designs look sharp on all products:

Resolution

Size Matters

  • Minimum 2800Ã—2800 pixels
  • 300 DPI resolution
  • Save as PNG for transparency
  • JPEG quality 90+ for photos
Formats

File Types

  • PNG: For graphics with transparency
  • JPEG: For photographs
  • PSD/AI: Keep layered originals
  • RGB color mode (not CMYK)
Organization

Naming Conventions

  • Use descriptive filenames
  • Include version numbers
  • Example: "Space_Cat_V2_4500px.png"
  • Keep backups of original files

Avoid These Common Mistakes

  • Upscaling small images (causes pixelation)
  • Using low-contrast color combinations
  • Placing critical elements near product edges
  • Forgetting to check different product previews

Device Cases

Color Theory for Print

Colors appear differently on various products and materials:

Palettes

Color Combinations

  • Use complementary colors for contrast
  • Limit palette to 3-5 main colors
  • Test grayscale for value contrast
  • Consider color psychology
Print Differences

Material Variations

  • Power Bank Pouches: Colors appear vibrant
  • Phone Pouches: Colors appear vibrant
  • Stickers: Most color-accurate

Accessibility Tips

  • Ensure sufficient color contrast (4.5:1 minimum)
  • Avoid color-only information (add patterns/text)
  • Test designs with color blindness simulators

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I make my designs stand out in search?

Optimize your listings with:

  1. Unique designs: Avoid oversaturated concepts
  2. Strategic tagging: 15-20 relevant tags
  3. Quality thumbnails: Clear, eye-catching previews
  4. Trend awareness: Timely themes
  5. Series: Multiple designs on popular themes

Can I use fonts in my designs?

Font usage rules:

  • Free fonts: Must be 100% free for commercial use
  • Paid fonts: Need proof of commercial license
  • Hand lettering: Always acceptable if original
  • Brand fonts: Never allowed (e.g., Disney font)

When in doubt, create original lettering.

Why do some colors look different on products?

Color variations occur because:

  • Different materials absorb inks differently
  • Monitor calibration varies between devices
  • Lighting affects color perception

Always check product previews and order samples.

How many designs should I upload weekly?

Recommended upload frequency:

  • Beginners: 3-5 quality designs weekly
  • Intermediate: 5-10 weekly
  • Advanced: 10-20 weekly (with team)

Quality always trumps quantity. Better to upload one great design than five mediocre ones.
